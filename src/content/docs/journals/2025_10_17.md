In numerology, an empty square in a Lo Shu grid indicates a missing number, which represents an area of life or a personality trait that may be lacking or present a challenge. The grid is populated with numbers from a person's date of birth, and any numbers from 1 to 9 that are not present are considered "missing".
The meaning of each empty square is determined by the specific number it represents.
The Lo Shu grid
The Lo Shu grid is a 3x3 magic square with fixed positions for the numbers 1 through 9:
4 9 2
3 5 7
8 1 6
Meaning of empty squares
Missing 1: Challenges with self-expression, confidence, and leadership. This can lead to relying too much on others and difficulty acting alone.
Missing 2: Issues with emotional balance, sensitivity, and cooperation. The person may lack patience and intuitive power.
Missing 3: Lack of creativity, self-expression, and confidence. This can make it difficult to think logically when faced with distractions.
Missing 4: Struggles with organization, discipline, and practicality. The person may find it difficult to stick to a routine and can lack courage.
Missing 5: Difficulty with stability, communication, and adaptability. This can manifest as a resistance to change and struggles in decision-making.
Missing 6: Problems related to family, luxury, and responsibility. The person may experience financial difficulties or have trouble expressing emotions openly.
Missing 7: Issues with spirituality, intuition, and introspection. This may result in anxiety and a lack of self-awareness and trust.
Missing 8: Challenges related to ambition, financial management, and motivation. A person with a missing 8 may lack drive and leave tasks unfinished.
Missing 9: Low motivation, lack of passion, and potential insensitivity to the needs of others. The person may struggle for recognition and name.
Empty planes and their interpretation
In addition to the individual numbers, numerologists also analyze "planes" or rows of the grid. An empty square in a particular plane indicates an "inactive" or "broken" plane, symbolizing a weakness in that area of life.
Mental Plane (4, 9, 2): If numbers are missing from this top row, it can indicate a weak memory, a lack of focus, and an impulsive nature.
Emotional Plane (3, 5, 7): Empty squares in the middle row suggest a person may feel lonely, unmotivated, or struggle with emotional balance.
Practical Plane (8, 1, 6): If numbers are missing from the bottom row, it can point to being impractical, living in a "dreamy world," or having poor financial judgment.
Will Plane (9, 5, 1): Empty squares in this central vertical column signify a lack of determination and willpower to see things through to completion.
Action Plane (2, 7, 6): If numbers are missing from this right vertical column, it can mean a person is indecisive, misses opportunities, and leaves tasks unfinished.
