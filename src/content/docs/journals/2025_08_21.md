🪔 श्रीशांतेरी लघु मानस पूजा
(चारुदत्त शास्त्री कोर्डे विरचितम्)

1. ध्यानम्

सिंहवाहिनीं त्रिनेत्रां शशांककान्तिविग्रहाम् ।  
रत्नमाल्यविभूषां तां शांतेरीं ध्यानमाहम् ॥

2. आवाहनम्

ॐ श्रीशांतेरी देव्यै नमः ।  
आगच्छ भगवति पूजार्थं मम गृहं सदा ॥

3. आसनं समर्पयामि

सुवर्णासनं कल्पितं ते देव्यै समर्पयामि ॥

4. पाद्यं, अर्घ्यं, आचमनं
   गङ्गाजलं पाद्यं समर्पयामि ।  
   चन्दनकुङ्कुमयुक्तं अर्घ्यं समर्पयामि ।  
   शीतलं जलं आचमनं समर्पयामि ॥

5. स्नानं
   सप्तसिन्धुजलैः स्नानं कुरुष्व भगवति ॥

6. वस्त्रं, आभरणं, गन्धं, पुष्पं
   रत्नवस्त्रं समर्पयामि ।  
   स्वर्णाभरणानि समर्पयामि ।  
   गन्धं च पुष्पं च समर्पयामि ॥

7. धूपं, दीपं, नैवेद्यं

सुगन्धिधूपं समर्पयामि ।  
प्रदीप्तदीपं समर्पयामि ।  
सर्वरससमेतं नैवेद्यं समर्पयामि ॥

8. मन्त्रपुष्पाञ्जलिः
   ॐ यं शांतेरीं पूजयामि भक्त्या ।  
   सर्वकामफलप्रदां नमामि ॥

9. प्रार्थना
   त्वं मातरः गोमंतकवासिनी ।  
   भक्तवत्सला शुभदायिनी ।  
   रक्ष मां सर्वदा शांतेरी ।  
   सर्वसिद्धिं देहि मे सदा ॥

10. नमस्कारः

ॐ श्रीशांतेरी देव्यै नमः ।  
नमस्ते नमस्ते नमस्ते ॥

Japanese philosophies emphasize harmony, impermanence, and personal purpose, while Indian philosophies explore metaphysics, ethics, and liberation through diverse schools of thought.

Here’s a curated list of major philosophies from both traditions:

---

🇯🇵 Japanese Philosophies

Japanese thought blends Shinto, Buddhist, Confucian, and indigenous aesthetics into everyday life. These philosophies often guide personal conduct, creativity, and mindfulness.

| Philosophy    | Meaning / Focus                                                             |
| ------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Ikigai        | Purpose of life; intersection of passion, mission, vocation, and profession |
| Wabi-Sabi     | Beauty in imperfection and impermanence                                     |
| Shinrin-yoku  | Forest bathing; healing through nature immersion                            |
| Kaizen        | Continuous improvement in small steps                                       |
| Kintsugi      | Repairing broken pottery with gold; embracing flaws                         |
| Gaman         | Endurance with dignity and patience                                         |
| Mottainai     | Regret over waste; respect for resources                                    |
| Oubaitori     | Avoiding comparison; blooming in one’s own season                           |
| Zanshin       | Mindfulness and awareness in action                                         |
| Mono no aware | Awareness of impermanence; gentle sadness                                   |

---

🇮🇳 Indian Philosophies

Indian philosophy spans millennia, with deep inquiry into consciousness, reality, and liberation. It includes both orthodox (āstika) and heterodox (nāstika) schools.

🕉 Orthodox (Āstika) Schools
| School | Focus / Key Concepts |
|------------------|-----------------------------------------------|
| Nyāya | Logic and epistemology |
| Vaiśeṣika | Atomism and metaphysics |
| Sāṅkhya | Dualism of puruṣa (consciousness) and prakṛti (matter) |
| Yoga | Discipline, meditation, and liberation |
| Mīmāṃsā | Rituals and dharma interpretation |
| Vedānta | Nature of Brahman and self; includes:

- Advaita (non-dualism)
- Dvaita (dualism)
- Viśiṣṭādvaita (qualified non-dualism) |

☸ Heterodox (Nāstika) Schools
| School | Focus / Key Concepts |
|------------------|-----------------------------------------------|
| Buddhism | Four Noble Truths, Eightfold Path, Anatta |
| Jainism | Non-violence, karma, and liberation through austerity |
| Cārvāka | Materialism and skepticism |
| Ājīvika | Determinism and niyati (fate) |
| Ajnāna | Radical skepticism; denial of knowledge |
